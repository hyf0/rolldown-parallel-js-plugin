# Scale-Crossover Protocol Amendment 8: Terminal Outcomes and Stage Resources

Status: frozen on 2026-07-13 before any formal scale, attribution, allocation, quota, or initialization timing child executes. This amendment preserves every corpus, scale grid, lifecycle-fixed wall runtime, host gate, statistical threshold, resource threshold, local-only rule, and semantic distinction from the frozen protocol and Amendments 1 through 7. It corrects result-terminal handling, narrows correctness provenance to its executable dependency closure, and supersedes the attribution-only runtime after stage-resource and post-close boundaries became available.

## Valid negative and non-monotonic outcomes

A one-shot base screen may be non-monotonic. It remains a selector rather than a conclusion, and the deterministic initial confirmation repeats the first direction interval, the adjacent base point, and the full scale. Repeated positive-to-negative evidence terminates as `non-monotonic-repeated-evidence` with the first observed reversal scale instead of throwing. Exact, left-censored, right-censored, interval-censored, non-monotonic, and no-crossover results are equally valid study outcomes; no evaluator or downstream allocation stage may require a favorable exact crossover merely to finish.

Independent Vue confirmation records `screenSelectionStatus: resource-envelope-eligible` when at least one screened worker passes the one-shot resource envelope. If none does, it records `screenSelectionStatus: no-resource-envelope-worker`, selects the fastest screened worker only to obtain repeated negative evidence, and still confirms ordinary, that worker, its adjacent in-range counts, fixed four, and fixed eight after deduplication. Absence of an eligible worker is evidence against automatic parallel execution, not a harness failure.

When the MDX resource crossover is not exact, the Rust-pool allocation and aggregate CPU-rate stages are `unavailable` and `not-applicable` with reason `resource-crossover-not-exact`; they bind the terminal crossover artifact and its actual status but launch no allocation, quota, or controller child. Fixed ordinary, worker-four, and worker-eight policy evaluation still consumes every repeated baseline block. This preserves a complete negative policy result without inventing a crossover point.

## Initialization derivation corrections

Pool `firstReadyMs` and `allReadyMs` use the common main-process `beforeWorkerPool` snapshot as their origin and worker `readyMessageAt` events as endpoints. `readySkewMs` is the last-ready event minus the first-ready event. Per-worker `mainReadyMs` retains its separate constructor-to-ready meaning and may not be mixed across workers as if all constructors started together.

The JavaScript factory aggregate must have a positive total and maximum, `maximum <= total <= factoryCalls * maximum`, and one ordinary factory requires total and maximum equality within the numeric tolerance. Derived evidence retains ordinary main plugin construction, ordinary factory total/maximum, worker proxy construction, worker factory total/maximum, pool first/all-ready, ready skew, and critical maxima for metrics-runtime import, runtime-and-binding import, implementation import, factory, bindingification, and registration. Stage maxima are parallel critical-worker observations and are never summed into a wall total.

## Product and policy limits

The executable product ledger classifies code, source maps, metadata, diagnostics, state, plugin context and side effects, hook order, lifecycle, virtual modules, deterministic worker-local caches, task failure, and shutdown as exactly `pass`, `product-failure`, or `not-tested`. Semantic capability completion alone is not a product crossover. A family reaches `productCrossover:true` only if every capability passes and both a repeated mechanical and resource-acceptable performance crossover are established from bound evidence.

The explicit aggregate CPU-rate formal axis remains MDX-only. It does not establish quota behavior across Vue, Svelte, or heterogeneous plugin families, and no final policy claim may describe it as cross-family validation. The unchanged runtime exposes fixed worker counts but no progressive selector that can change placement safely after observing arrivals; ready-queue, arrival-history, and throughput-based online calibration therefore remain unvalidated and unavailable rather than inferred from the fixed-count traces. Machine topology may define a process safety cap but cannot select an optimum by itself.

The already bound full-corpus and semantic MDX failures make product crossover impossible for the current adapter. Intermediate graph correctness at 512, 2,048, or crossover-neighbor scales is not required to decide the mechanical/resource transform curve and will not be run merely to repeat that known product failure; `graph-formal-matrix.json` remains disabled. This narrows only the graph-product follow-up, not the 32-to-9,157 nested transform curve, full-corpus correctness, semantic sentinel, or attribution requirements.

The current host remains ineligible because uptime and swap exceed their frozen limits. This amendment authorizes no timing result by itself. A later change to these terminal statuses, fallback selection, attribution runtime, stage schema, correctness closure, product capability set, evidence store, corpus, scale, threshold, or host rule requires another versioned amendment before affected execution.
